1 System Description
1.1 Background and Problem Statement
Gulu University relies heavily on privately-owned community hostels to house its stu-
dent population. Because the university cannot accommodate all students on-campus,
thousands of students — many travelling from across Uganda or from across interna-
tional borders — must independently navigate the local housing market upon admission
or transfer. Conventional roommate selection methods, including word-of-mouth re-
ferrals, social media listings, and random assignments, consistently fail to account
for socio-behavioural compatibility. Mismatches in lifestyle variables such as sleep
schedules, study habits, cleanliness standards, and social preferences generate persistent
interpersonal conflict, which adversely affects academic performance and overall student
wellbeing (Kigundu, 2014). This situation is further at odds with the obligation imposed
on higher education institutions by theUniversities and Other Tertiary Institutions
Act (Republic of Uganda, 2001)to maintain a conducive environment for learning.

1.2 Proposed Solution
LinkStay is a centralised, browser-based web portal designed to replace the trial-and-
error roommate selection process with a transparent, data-driven matching system.
The portal collects structured lifestyle preference data from students through an
onboarding survey and applies a two-tier algorithmic engine to generate compatibility-
based roommate recommendations. In the first tier, aWeighted Sum Model (WSM)
quantifies the degree of lifestyle alignment between every pair of registered students
by computing a weighted composite score across key behavioural variables. In the
second tier, theGale-Shapley Stable Matching Algorithm(Gale & Shapley, 1962)
processes those scores as preference rankings to produce a set of mathematically stable
pairings — pairings in which no two students would mutually prefer an alternative
arrangement over the one assigned by the system. The result is a roommate allocation
process grounded in mathematical stability and socio-behavioural alignment rather
than chance.
1.3 System Scope
LinkStay is geographically scoped to students residing in gazetted private hostels within
Gulu City and is functionally limited to compatibility-based roommate matching. The
system does not extend to rent payment processing, landlord property administration,
or full hostel management operations. It is a standalone web application and does not
integrate with Gulu University’s existing academic management systems in this first
release.
1.4 Users of the System
The system serves three distinct user classes.Student usersare the primary consumers
of the matching service; they register, complete the preference survey, and receive
ranked roommate recommendations.The system administratoris a designated
university staff member or project team member responsible for monitoring registrations,
managing accounts, and overseeing matching runs via an administrative dashboard.
Academic supervisors and evaluatorsinteract with the system through summary
reports and dashboard views to assess algorithmic performance and system outcomes.
1.5 Technology Overview
LinkStay is built on thePythonprogramming language using theDjangoweb
framework, with aMySQLrelational database for persistent storage. The frontend
usesHTML5,CSS3,Bootstrap 5, andJavaScriptto deliver a responsive, low-
bandwidth-friendly interface accessible from any modern web browser. Development
follows theAgile Scrummethodology with iterative sprints, enabling continuous
refinement of both the interface and the matching algorithm throughout the project
lifecycle.
